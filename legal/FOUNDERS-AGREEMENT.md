## ARTICLE 1: THE PROJECT

### 1.1 Project Description
The Project, known as "State Spots" (formerly "Michigan Spots"), is a comprehensive geolocation gaming platform that includes:
- Web application (Astro + React)
- Reddit Devvit integration
- AI-powered business discovery features
- Partnership management system
- Multi-tier subscription model
- All associated intellectual property, source code, databases, designs, and business models

### 1.2 Current Operating Structure
The Project currently operates as a division of Cozyartz Media Group, a Michigan business entity. The Founders acknowledge that State Spots may be spun off into a separate legal entity (LLC, C-Corp, or other structure) at a future date to be mutually agreed upon.

### 1.3 Business Purpose
The Project's purpose is to create a sustainable, scalable geolocation discovery platform that generates revenue through:
- Chamber of Commerce partnerships
- Local business sponsorships
- Premium subscriptions
- Commercial licensing
- Potential future investment or acquisition

## ARTICLE 3: DECISION-MAKING AUTHORITY

### 3.1 Day-to-Day Decisions
Each Founder has autonomous authority within their defined areas of responsibility for routine operational decisions that do not exceed $500 in cost or significantly alter the product roadmap.

### 3.2 Major Decisions Requiring Unanimous Approval
The following decisions require **unanimous approval** of both Founders:

**Financial Decisions**:
- Expenditures exceeding $2,000
- Taking on debt or loans
- Granting equity or ownership interests
- Establishing banking relationships
- Tax elections and accounting methods

**Strategic Decisions**:
- Pivoting the business model
- Changing the core product offering
- Entering new markets or verticals
- Pursuing acquisition opportunities
- Selling or licensing major intellectual property
- Establishing partnerships with revenue share > $10,000/year

**Personnel Decisions**:
- Hiring full-time employees
- Engaging contractors for projects > $5,000
- Terminating employees or key contractors

**Legal & Compliance**:
- Changing corporate structure
- Entering material contracts (> $5,000 value)
- Settling legal disputes or lawsuits
- Changing intellectual property licensing
- Engaging outside legal counsel

### 3.3 Deadlock Resolution
If the Founders cannot reach unanimous agreement on a Major Decision within 14 days of discussion:

**Step 1: Mediation** (Days 15-30)
Engage a mutually agreed-upon business mediator to facilitate discussion.

**Step 2: Advisory Board Vote** (Days 31-45)
If mediation fails, present the issue to an Advisory Board consisting of:
- One advisor selected by Andrea
- One advisor selected by Amy
- One neutral third-party advisor mutually agreed upon

The Advisory Board's recommendation (by majority vote) is **advisory only**, not binding.

**Step 3: Buyout Option** (After Day 45)
If no resolution is reached, either Founder may trigger the Buyout process described in Article 8.

### 4.2 Vesting Schedule

To ensure long-term commitment, all Founder equity is subject to a **4-year vesting schedule** with a **1-year cliff**:

- **Year 1 (Cliff)**: 0% vested until 12 months from Effective Date
- **After Cliff**: 25% vested immediately upon reaching 12-month anniversary
- **Years 2-4**: Remaining 75% vests monthly over 36 months (2.08%/month)

**Example**:
If Andrea holds 50% equity:
- Month 1-11: 0% vested (0% owned if she leaves)
- Month 12: 12.5% vested (25% of her 50%)
- Month 13: 13.54% vested
- Month 48: 50% fully vested

### 4.3 Acceleration Provisions

**Single-Trigger Acceleration**:
If State Spots is acquired for more than $1,000,000, all unvested equity accelerates by 25%.

**Double-Trigger Acceleration**:
If a Founder is terminated without cause within 12 months of an acquisition, all of that Founder's unvested equity immediately vests 100%.

### 4.4 Dilution Protection
If State Spots issues new equity (e.g., for investors or employees), Founders' ownership percentages will dilute proportionally. However, Founders have the right of first refusal to purchase new shares to maintain their current ownership percentage.

### 7.2 Non-Compete (Post-Exit - 12 Months)
For 12 months after a Founder exits the Project, that Founder agrees not to:
- Launch a directly competing product targeting the same market
- Solicit State Spots customers, partners, or employees for a competing venture
- Use State Spots trade secrets in a competitive manner

**Geographic Scope**: United States
**Reasonable Limitation**: This non-compete applies only to geolocation-based discovery/gaming platforms; it does not prevent the Founder from working in other areas of technology.

### 7.3 Non-Solicitation of Team Members
For 18 months after exit, a departing Founder agrees not to solicit, recruit, or hire any employee, contractor, or advisor who worked with State Spots within the prior 12 months.

### 7.4 Acknowledgment of Reasonableness
The Founders acknowledge that these restrictions are reasonable in scope, duration, and geography, and are necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests.

## ARTICLE 8: FOUNDER EXIT & BUYOUT

### 8.1 Voluntary Exit
A Founder may voluntarily exit the Project by providing 60 days' written notice. Upon exit:
- **Vested Equity**: Founder retains all vested equity
- **Unvested Equity**: All unvested equity is forfeited
- **Repurchase Right**: Company has the right (but not obligation) to repurchase vested equity at Fair Market Value within 90 days

### 8.2 Involuntary Termination (For Cause)
A Founder may be removed "For Cause" by the other Founder if they:
- Commit fraud, embezzlement, or theft
- Breach fiduciary duties
- Materially breach this Agreement (and fail to cure within 30 days)
- Are convicted of a felony
- Engage in conduct that materially harms the Company's reputation

**Consequences**:
- **Vested Equity**: Company may repurchase at 50% of Fair Market Value
- **Unvested Equity**: Forfeited immediately
- **No Severance**: No compensation owed

### 8.3 Involuntary Termination (Without Cause)
If a Founder is removed without cause:
- **Vested Equity**: Founder retains; Company may repurchase at Fair Market Value
- **Unvested Equity**: 25% of unvested equity accelerates; remainder forfeited
- **Severance**: 3 months of average monthly distributions (if any)

### 8.4 Death or Permanent Disability
If a Founder dies or becomes permanently disabled (unable to perform duties for 6+ months):
- **Vested Equity**: Passes to Founder's estate/heirs; subject to repurchase at Fair Market Value
- **Unvested Equity**: 50% accelerates; remainder forfeited
- **Life Insurance**: Company encouraged to maintain key person life insurance

### 8.5 Fair Market Value Determination
Fair Market Value shall be determined by:
1. **Mutual Agreement**: Founders agree on valuation, OR
2. **Recent Funding**: Most recent third-party investment valuation (within 12 months), OR
3. **Independent Appraisal**: Mutually selected business appraiser (cost split 50/50)
