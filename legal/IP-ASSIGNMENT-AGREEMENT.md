## ARTICLE 1: ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 1.1 Work Product
All work product, inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, writings, designs, ideas, and other intellectual property (collectively, "Work Product") that Assignor creates, conceives, develops, or reduces to practice, either alone or jointly with others, during the period of engagement with the Company that:

(a) Relates to the Company's actual or anticipated business, research, or development; OR
(b) Results from any work performed for the Company; OR
(c) Is created using the Company's equipment, facilities, materials, trade secrets, or confidential information; OR
(d) Relates to the State Spots platform in any way

is hereby irrevocably assigned to the Company as "work made for hire" under copyright law, or if not qualifying as work made for hire, is hereby assigned to the Company in perpetuity.

### 1.2 Past Work Product
Assignor also assigns to the Company all Work Product created prior to the Effective Date that:
(a) Was created specifically for the State Spots platform; OR
(b) Has been incorporated into the State Spots codebase or business operations; OR
(c) Is listed in Exhibit A (Prior Work to Be Assigned)

### 1.3 Retained Prior Inventions
Assignor has disclosed in **Exhibit B (Prior Inventions Retained)** all inventions, works, and intellectual property that:
- Assignor created before the Effective Date
- Assignor wishes to retain ownership of
- Will NOT be used in State Spots

Any work not listed in Exhibit B is presumed to be assigned to the Company under this Agreement.

### 1.4 Scope of Assignment
The assignment under this Agreement includes all worldwide rights, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including without limitation:

**Copyrights**:
- All copyrights, copyright registrations, and copyright applications
- All moral rights, rights of attribution, and integrity rights (to the extent waivable)
- All rights to reproduce, distribute, display, perform, and create derivative works

**Patents**:
- All patents, patent applications, and provisional applications
- All continuations, divisions, continuations-in-part, reissues, and reexaminations
- All improvements, enhancements, and modifications

**Trademarks**:
- All trademarks, service marks, trade names, and logos
- All trademark registrations and applications
- All goodwill associated with the marks

**Trade Secrets**:
- All trade secrets, proprietary information, and know-how
- All confidential business methods and processes
- All proprietary algorithms and technical specifications

**Other IP Rights**:
- All moral rights, database rights, and design rights
- All domain names, social media handles, and digital assets
- All rights in any jurisdiction worldwide, whether now known or hereafter developed

## ARTICLE 2: OBLIGATIONS OF ASSIGNOR

### 2.1 Disclosure
Assignor shall promptly and fully disclose to the Company all Work Product created during the engagement, whether or not Assignor believes it is subject to this Agreement.

### 2.2 Documentation
Assignor shall:
(a) Maintain adequate written records of all Work Product;
(b) Provide the Company with all documentation, source code, designs, and other materials related to Work Product;
(c) Execute any documents reasonably requested by the Company to perfect or enforce its IP rights, including patent applications, copyright registrations, and trademark filings.

### 2.3 Cooperation
Assignor agrees to cooperate with the Company (at Company's expense) to:
(a) Execute assignments, declarations, affidavits, and other documents;
(b) Provide testimony in legal proceedings related to the Work Product;
(c) Assist with patent prosecution, trademark registration, or copyright enforcement;
(d) Take any other action reasonably necessary to vest complete ownership in the Company.

### 2.4 Post-Engagement Cooperation
Assignor's obligations under Section 2.3 survive termination of engagement with the Company. If the Company cannot locate Assignor after reasonable effort, the Company is hereby appointed as Assignor's attorney-in-fact to execute documents on Assignor's behalf.

### 2.5 Return of Materials
Upon termination of engagement or upon request, Assignor shall promptly return to the Company all:
- Documents, files, and data (in any format)
- Equipment, devices, and tools
- Source code, prototypes, and designs
- Confidential information and trade secrets
- Any copies (physical or digital) of the above
