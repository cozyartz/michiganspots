## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 Confidential Information Defined
"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection of tangible objects, including without limitation:

**(a) Technical Information**:
- Source code, object code, algorithms, and software architecture
- Database schemas, data models, and technical specifications
- Product designs, prototypes, and engineering drawings
- Research and development projects
- Technical know-how, processes, and formulas
- System architecture and infrastructure details

**(b) Business Information**:
- Business plans, strategies, and financial projections
- Revenue models, pricing structures, and cost data
- Marketing plans and market research
- Customer lists, partner agreements, and vendor relationships
- Sales forecasts and business metrics
- Roadmaps and product launch plans

**(c) Proprietary Information**:
- Trade secrets and know-how
- Inventions and patent applications (whether or not patentable)
- Copyrighted works and creative content
- Trademarks, branding, and marketing materials

**(d) Other Information**:
- Any information marked "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation
- Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure

### 1.2 Form of Disclosure
Confidential Information may be disclosed in any form, including:
- Written or printed documents
- Electronic files (email, cloud storage, source code repositories)
- Oral communications (meetings, calls, presentations)
- Visual inspections of facilities, equipment, or prototypes
- Screen shares, demonstrations, or software access

###1.3 Marking Requirement
Confidential Information disclosed in tangible form shall be marked "Confidential" or with a similar legend. Confidential Information disclosed orally shall be identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing to the Receiving Party within 30 days.

## ARTICLE 2: EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

**(a)** Was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

**(b)** Was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;

**(c)** Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

**(d)** Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation;

**(e)** Is disclosed by the Disclosing Party to a third party without restrictions on disclosure;

**(f)** Is approved for release by written authorization of the Disclosing Party.

## ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure
The Receiving Party agrees to:
(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent;
(c) Not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose defined in the Recitals ("Permitted Purpose").

### 3.2 Standard of Care
The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

### 3.3 Limited Disclosure to Representatives
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, contractors, advisors, and representatives (collectively, "Representatives") who:
(a) Have a legitimate need to know the information for the Permitted Purpose;
(b) Have been informed of the confidential nature of the information;
(c) Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained in this Agreement.

The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

### 3.4 Secure Storage
The Receiving Party shall:
(a) Store Confidential Information in secure locations with restricted access;
(b) Use password protection, encryption, and other reasonable security measures for electronic Confidential Information;
(c) Limit access to physical documents containing Confidential Information;
(d) Not remove any confidential markings or legends.

### 3.5 No Reverse Engineering
The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, underlying ideas, algorithms, or structure from any Confidential Information.

## ARTICLE 4: COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

### 4.1 Legal Requirements
If the Receiving Party is legally compelled (by court order, subpoena, government agency request, or operation of law) to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) Provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement (if legally permitted);
(b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party's efforts to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy;
(c) Disclose only the minimum Confidential Information necessary to comply with the legal requirement;
(d) Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment of the disclosed information.

### 4.2 Costs
The Disclosing Party shall bear all costs associated with seeking a protective order or contesting the disclosure requirement. The Receiving Party shall reasonably cooperate at the Disclosing Party's expense.

### 5.2 No License

### 5.3 No Obligation to Disclose
Neither Party is obligated to disclose any particular Confidential Information. The Disclosing Party may disclose information in its sole discretion.

### 5.4 No Obligation to Proceed

## ARTICLE 6: RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF INFORMATION

### 6.1 Upon Request or Termination
Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party's option:

**(a) Return**:
Promptly return all Confidential Information in tangible form (including copies, notes, and derivatives); OR

**(b) Destroy**:
Destroy all Confidential Information (including electronic copies, notes, and derivatives) and certify such destruction in writing.

### 6.2 Exceptions
The Receiving Party may retain:
(a) One archival copy of Confidential Information for legal compliance purposes, provided it remains subject to this Agreement's confidentiality obligations;
(b) Electronic copies stored in automatic backup systems, provided such copies are not accessed and are destroyed in the ordinary course of business.

### 6.3 No Obligation to Delete Residual Knowledge
This Agreement does not require the Receiving Party to erase from memory or cease using general skills, knowledge, or experience that employees or representatives may have retained.
