Must-visits: <strong>Selden Standard</strong> (seasonal small plates), <strong>Lady of the House</strong> (nose-to-tail dining), and <strong>Takoi</strong> (Thai street food). Don't leave without trying a classic Detroit-style coney dog from Lafayette or American Coney Island‚Äîlocals still debate which is better!</p>

    <h2>2. Ann Arbor: Farm-to-Table Pioneer</h2>
    <p>Ann Arbor was doing farm-to-table before it was trendy. <strong>Zingerman's Delicatessen</strong> is legendary (try the Reuben‚Äîseriously), while <strong>Zingerman's Roadhouse</strong> serves elevated comfort food with ingredients sourced within 100 miles. The Ann Arbor Farmers Market (Wednesdays and Saturdays) showcases the region's incredible agricultural bounty.</p>

    <h2>3. Traverse City: Cherry Capital & Wine Country</h2>
    <p>Traverse City isn't just cherries‚Äîthough you MUST try cherry pie at <strong>Grand Traverse Pie Company</strong>. The Leelanau and Old Mission Peninsulas boast 40+ wineries, many with stunning bay views. Food highlights: <strong>Trattoria Stella</strong> (Italian in a former asylum), <strong>Mama Lu's</strong> (tacos and tequila), and <strong>Red Spire Brunch</strong> (worth the wait).</p>

    <h2>4. The Upper Peninsula: Pasty Paradise</h2>
    <p>The UP's mining heritage lives on through the pasty‚Äîa hearty meat-and-vegetable turnover that's pure comfort food. Authentic pasties from <strong>Lawry's Pasty Shop</strong> (Ishpeming) or <strong>Muldoon's Pasties</strong> (Munising) are mandatory. Pair with a local craft beer and you've experienced UP culture at its finest. Bonus: Try whitefish from Lake Superior‚Äîit's incredibly fresh and delicious!</p>

    <h2>5. Grand Rapids: Craft Beer Capital of Michigan</h2>
    <p>With 80+ breweries, Grand Rapids earns its "Beer City USA" title. <strong>Founders Brewing</strong>, <strong>New Holland</strong>, and <strong>Brewery Vivant</strong> lead the pack, but don't skip smaller gems like <strong>Aperitivo</strong> and <strong>Harmony Hall</strong>. Food scene standouts: <strong>The Sovengard</strong> (Scandinavian), <strong>San Chez</strong> (tapas), and <strong>Reserve Wine & Food</strong> (upscale American).</p>

    <h2>6. Leelanau Peninsula: Artisan Food & Wine Trail</h2>
    <p>This scenic peninsula is a foodie's dream road trip. Hit <strong>9 Bean Rows</strong> for artisan coffee, <strong>Tandem Ciders</strong> for small-batch hard cider, <strong>Suttons Bay Ciders</strong> for more cider varieties, and <strong>Grocer's Daughter Chocolate</strong> for incredible handmade chocolates. Pack a picnic from <strong>Leelanau Cheese Company</strong> and enjoy sunset at a winery‚Äîabsolute perfection!</p>

    <h2>7. Holland: Dutch Heritage & Modern Cuisine</h2>
    <p>Holland blends traditional Dutch treats with contemporary dining. <strong>De Boer Bakkerij</strong> serves authentic Dutch pastries and stroopwafels, while <strong>New Holland Brewing</strong> offers craft beer and elevated pub fare. <strong>Curragh Irish Pub</strong> and <strong>Alpen Rose</strong> round out Holland's surprisingly diverse food scene. Don't miss tulip-shaped cookies from <strong>Good Goods</strong>!</p>

    <h2>Insider Tips for Michigan Food Tourism</h2>
    <div class="bg-lakes-blue/10 border-2 border-lakes-blue/30 rounded-lg p-4 my-6">
      <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 text-sm text-ink-secondary">
        <li><strong>Reservations:</strong> Popular restaurants (especially Traverse City in summer) book weeks in advance. Use OpenTable or call ahead.</li>
        <li><strong>Farmers Markets:</strong> Most operate Saturday mornings May-October. Bring cash‚Äîmany vendors don't take cards.</li>
        <li><strong>Brewery Tours:</strong> Most offer free tours on weekends. Call ahead to confirm times.</li>
        <li><strong>Wine Tasting:</strong> Leelanau/Old Mission wineries charge $5-15 for tastings (usually waived with purchase).</li>
        <li><strong>Food Halls:</strong> Detroit's Finn Hall and Grand Rapids' Downtown Market offer multiple vendors under one roof‚Äîperfect for groups with different tastes!</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
