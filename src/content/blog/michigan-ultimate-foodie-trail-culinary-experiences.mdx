    <h2>2. Ann Arbor: Farm-to-Table Pioneer</h2>

    <p>Ann Arbor was doing farm-to-table before it was trendy. The city's culinary scene focuses on locally-sourced ingredients and Michigan agricultural connections.</p>

    <p><strong>Zingerman's Delicatessen</strong> is legendary (try the Reuben‚Äîseriously). For dinner, <strong>Zingerman's Roadhouse</strong> serves elevated comfort food with ingredients sourced within 100 miles.</p>

    <p>The Ann Arbor Farmers Market (Wednesdays and Saturdays) showcases the region's incredible agricultural bounty. It's the perfect place to experience Michigan's farm culture.</p>

    <h2>3. Traverse City: Cherry Capital & Wine Country</h2>

    <p>Traverse City isn't just cherries‚Äîthough you MUST try cherry pie at <strong>Grand Traverse Pie Company</strong>. The city has evolved into a sophisticated food and wine destination.</p>

    <p>The Leelanau and Old Mission Peninsulas boast 40+ wineries, many with stunning Grand Traverse Bay views. Wine tastings with sunset views are unforgettable.</p>

    <p><strong>Food highlights:</strong></p>
    <ul class="space-y-2 mb-4">
      <li><strong>Trattoria Stella</strong> - Upscale Italian in a beautifully converted former asylum</li>
      <li><strong>Mama Lu's</strong> - Creative tacos and extensive tequila selection</li>
      <li><strong>Red Spire Brunch</strong> - Worth the weekend wait for creative brunch dishes</li>
    </ul>

    <p>The UP's mining heritage lives on through the pasty‚Äîa hearty meat-and-vegetable turnover that's pure comfort food. This Cornish import sustained miners and remains UP's signature dish.</p>

    <p>Authentic pasties from <strong>Lawry's Pasty Shop</strong> (Ishpeming) or <strong>Muldoon's Pasties</strong> (Munising) are mandatory. Each shop has secret family recipes passed down for generations.</p>

    <p>Pair your pasty with a local craft beer and you've experienced UP culture at its finest. <strong>Bonus:</strong> Try whitefish from Lake Superior‚Äîit's incredibly fresh and delicious!</p>

    <h2>5. Grand Rapids: Craft Beer Capital of Michigan</h2>

    <p>With 80+ breweries, Grand Rapids earns its "Beer City USA" title. The city has transformed into Michigan's beer mecca.</p>

    <p><strong>Top breweries:</strong> <strong>Founders Brewing</strong>, <strong>New Holland</strong>, and <strong>Brewery Vivant</strong> lead the pack. Don't skip smaller gems like <strong>Aperitivo</strong> and <strong>Harmony Hall</strong>.</p>

    <p><strong>Food scene standouts:</strong></p>
    <ul class="space-y-2 mb-4">
      <li><strong>The Sovengard</strong> - Inventive Scandinavian cuisine</li>
      <li><strong>San Chez</strong> - Spanish tapas and extensive wine list</li>
      <li><strong>Reserve Wine & Food</strong> - Upscale American with seasonal menus</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>6. Leelanau Peninsula: Artisan Food & Wine Trail</h2>

    <p>This scenic peninsula is a foodie's dream road trip. Rolling hills, lakeside views, and artisan producers make every stop memorable.</p>

    <p><strong>Must-visit artisan producers:</strong></p>
    <ul class="space-y-2 mb-4">
      <li><strong>9 Bean Rows</strong> - Exceptional artisan coffee with beans roasted on-site</li>
      <li><strong>Tandem Ciders</strong> - Small-batch hard cider from Michigan apples</li>
      <li><strong>Suttons Bay Ciders</strong> - More cider varieties and scenic tasting room</li>
      <li><strong>Grocer's Daughter Chocolate</strong> - Incredible handmade chocolates worth the trip alone</li>
    </ul>

    <p>Pack a picnic from <strong>Leelanau Cheese Company</strong> and enjoy sunset at a winery‚Äîabsolute perfection!</p>

    <h2>7. Holland: Dutch Heritage & Modern Cuisine</h2>

    <p>Holland blends traditional Dutch treats with contemporary dining. The city's Dutch heritage shines through in bakeries and restaurants.</p>

    <p><strong>De Boer Bakkerij</strong> serves authentic Dutch pastries and stroopwafels‚Äîperfect for breakfast or afternoon treats.</p>

    <p><strong>New Holland Brewing</strong> offers craft beer and elevated pub fare in a welcoming atmosphere. Their outdoor patio is perfect for summer dining.</p>

    <p><strong>Curragh Irish Pub</strong> and <strong>Alpen Rose</strong> round out Holland's surprisingly diverse food scene. Don't miss tulip-shaped cookies from <strong>Good Goods</strong>!</p>

    <h2>Michigan Food Festivals Worth Planning Around</h2>

    <p>Time your visit with Michigan's incredible food festivals for an even better experience. These events celebrate Michigan's culinary culture:</p>

    <p><strong>Must-attend events:</strong></p>
    <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 text-ink-secondary mb-6">
      <li><strong>National Cherry Festival</strong> (Traverse City, July) - Week-long cherry celebration with tastings, concerts, and parades</li>
      <li><strong>Michigan Brewers Guild Fall Beer Festival</strong> (Ypsilanti, October) - 100+ Michigan breweries in one location</li>
      <li><strong>Mackinac Island Lilac Festival</strong> (June) - Includes wine tastings and gourmet dining events</li>
      <li><strong>Detroit Fall Beer Festival</strong> (Eastern Market, October) - Craft beer in Detroit's iconic market</li>
      <li><strong>Kalamazoo Beer Week</strong> (January) - Week-long craft beer takeover with special releases</li>
      <li><strong>UP Pasty Fest</strong> (Calumet, June) - Pasty baking competition and Upper Peninsula culture celebration</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Insider Tips for Michigan Food Tourism</h2>
    <div class="bg-lakes-blue/10 border-2 border-lakes-blue/30 rounded-lg p-4 my-6">
      <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 text-sm text-ink-secondary">
        <li><strong>Reservations:</strong> Popular restaurants (especially Traverse City in summer) book weeks in advance. Use OpenTable or call ahead.</li>
        <li><strong>Farmers Markets:</strong> Most operate Saturday mornings May-October. Bring cash‚Äîmany vendors don't take cards.</li>
        <li><strong>Brewery Tours:</strong> Most offer free tours on weekends. Call ahead to confirm times.</li>
        <li><strong>Wine Tasting:</strong> Leelanau/Old Mission wineries charge $5-15 for tastings (usually waived with purchase).</li>
        <li><strong>Food Halls:</strong> Detroit's Finn Hall and Grand Rapids' Downtown Market offer multiple vendors under one roof‚Äîperfect for groups with different tastes!</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
