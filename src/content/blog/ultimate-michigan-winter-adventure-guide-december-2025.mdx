    <h2>2. Skiing & Snowboarding: Boyne Highlands & Nub's Nob</h2>

    <p>Northern Michigan's ski resorts rival anywhere in the Midwest. Boyne Highlands offers 55 runs, night skiing, and terrain parks for all levels. Nub's Nob features the longest vertical drop in Michigan's Lower Peninsula.</p>

    <p>Both have excellent snow-making systems ensuring great conditions all season.</p>

    <h2>3. Ice Castles at Lake Geneva (Day Trip from Michigan)</h2>

    <p>Just over the border in Wisconsin, the Lake Geneva Ice Castles are worth the 4-hour drive from Detroit. Walk through frozen tunnels, explore ice slides, and witness LED-lit frozen sculptures covering an acre.</p>

    <p>Open Friday-Sunday through February‚Äîthis is Instagram gold and an unforgettable experience!</p>

    <h2>4. Dog Sledding Adventures in the Upper Peninsula</h2>

    <p>Experience the thrill of mushing your own dog sled team! Nature's Kennel in McMillan offers half-day and full-day dog sledding experiences through pristine UP forests. You'll learn mushing commands, help harness the dogs, and take the reins yourself.</p>

    <p>Advance booking required‚Äîthis sells out fast!</p>

    <h2>6. Ice Fishing on Higgins Lake</h2>

    <p>Join Michigan's beloved winter tradition! Higgins Lake offers excellent ice fishing for perch, pike, and lake trout. Local outfitters provide heated ice shanties, equipment rentals, and even guide services.</p>

    <p>No experience necessary‚Äîguides teach you everything from drilling holes to catching your first fish through the ice.</p>

    <h2>7. Snowmobiling: Cadillac to Traverse City Trail</h2>

    <p>Michigan boasts 6,000+ miles of groomed snowmobile trails. The Cadillac to Traverse City route offers scenic forest riding, trail-side restaurants, and perfect UP-style winter adventure.</p>

    <p>Rent snowmobiles in Cadillac or Traverse City‚Äîmany places offer full-day and weekend packages.</p>

    <h2>8. Winter Camping at Porcupine Mountains</h2>

    <p>For the truly adventurous! The Porcupine Mountains Wilderness State Park offers winter camping in yurts and rustic cabins. Experience complete solitude, star-filled night skies, and the possibility of witnessing the Northern Lights.</p>

    <p>Limited availability‚Äîbook months in advance!</p>

    <h2>Winter Safety Essentials</h2>

    <p><strong>Before you head out, always:</strong></p>
    <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2 text-ink-secondary">
      <li>Check weather forecasts (Lake Superior generates intense squalls)</li>
      <li>Tell someone your plans and expected return time</li>
      <li>Pack emergency supplies: hand warmers, extra food, first aid kit</li>
      <li>Download offline maps (cell service is spotty in remote areas)</li>
      <li>Dress in layers with moisture-wicking base layers</li>
      <li>Bring more water than you think‚Äîwinter dehydration is real!</li>
    </ul>
